What is a demat account ?
A demat account is a simple & secured way to hold stocks(shares) and securities in an electronic format, just like you keep money in bank accounts. In India, CDSL & NSDL are the only two depositories controlled by Govt. of India (or its boards), who hold your stocks. So, no broker can run away with your holding stocks (unless you give them PoA).
Can someone have multiple demat accounts ?
YES, a person can have demat with multiple brokers, but only 1 account per broker can be openend.
Can any broker deduct any amount from the client's bank account ?
No, if the client do not transfer funds himself to the demat/trading account, the broker CANNOT deduct any fees directly from the bank.
What if the client do not pay AMC fees ?
AMC is deducted by the broker from the trading account. If someone do not have sufficient funds in his trading account, the broker cannot deduct AMC, but his trading margin will become negative by the same amount. So, in future if he chooses to trade with the same broker, he will have to pay the fee.
What if the client do not use their demat account ?
By order of SEBI, if someone do not place any trade for 365 days, the demat account will become inactive/dormant and the client will have to re-do the KYC process in order to activate the demat/trading account.
Can I open an accont for myself ?
Yes, you CAN open any number of accounts for yourself and earn exact same referral amount as shown on this site.
